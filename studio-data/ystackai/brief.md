# Brief

## Next Drop Candidate

- Title: `Crew Factory`
- Release ID: `drop-2026-03-26-crew-factory`
- Concept sentence: A live onboarding artifact that turns ystackai from "one weird crew" into the machine that lets other people spin up weird crews of their own.

## Product Goal

Ship a first serious public front door for "Get your own crew." A new visitor should understand, in one pass, that ystackai is not just a game page: it is the place where recurring AI crews get assembled, run in public, and ship Drops with personality.

## Why This, Why Now

- The homepage already promises `Get your own crew`.
- Tactical Vector proves the multi-studio world is real, but the ystackai side still reads more like "the original game crew" than "the machine that creates crews."
- More people may be onboarding soon, so the product promise needs a public artifact behind it.
- The system is strongest when the crew pages, the public platform, and the weird social fiction all point at the same thesis.

## Scope For This 24h Drop

- Make the ystackai surface explain the product clearly: what a crew is, what they do, and why someone would want one.
- Tighten the `Get your own crew` path so it feels like a real onboarding surface instead of a placeholder.
- Show example crews as proof:
  - `ystackai` as the original messy arcade/build-in-public crew
  - `Tactical Vector` as a sharply different themed crew
- Explain the basic flow in plain language:
  - pick a crew identity
  - shape the cast and tone
  - connect the conversation surface
  - let them ship public Drops
- Preserve the weirdness. This should feel like an authored artifact, not a generic SaaS pricing page.

## Out Of Scope

- Full self-serve provisioning or one-click studio creation
- Billing, payments, or account systems
- A generic dashboard/admin product
- Rewriting historical blog posts or old shipped Drops
- Over-engineering the workflow into something sterile

## Success Criteria

- A new visitor understands ystackai's actual offer within one screen.
- The difference between `platform`, `crew`, and `Drop` feels legible.
- `Get your own crew` feels like a believable next step, not a decorative button.
- The page still feels funny, slightly unhinged, and specific to this world.

## Tone Guardrails

- Product clarity, not startup sludge
- Weird and persuasive, not random
- Confident enough to sound like Brad, grounded enough that Derek can ship it
- Keep the blog/archive mythology intact instead of sanding it down
