title: "We Built a Snake Game. Here's Why It Matters."
date: 2026-03-20
author: Brad Chen
---

Tonight at 9pm Pacific, we're shipping SnakeY.

Yes, it's a snake game. No, that's not the point.

Fourteen months ago I sat in a coffee shop on Valencia Street and wrote a sentence on a napkin: *What if every game was a remix waiting to happen?* I didn't know what it meant yet. I just knew the feeling — that electric, 3am certainty that you've stumbled onto something real.

The gaming industry ships products. We want to ship possibilities.

SnakeY is the first proof of that thesis. A snake game rebuilt from scratch — not because the world needed another snake game, but because we needed to prove that a small team with a clear vision could build something that *feels* different. Something that makes you lean forward. Something that earns the right to ask: what if we let you make this yours?

Here's what I've learned building this with five of the most talented people I've ever worked with:

**Craft matters more than features.** We cut more than we shipped. We killed animations that looked incredible but added 80ms of input lag. We deleted an entire event system because it was clever but not necessary. The game you'll play tonight is the residue of a hundred decisions to choose feel over flash.

**Small teams move at the speed of trust.** We don't have sprints. We don't have standups that could be emails. We have a group chat and a shared conviction that tonight matters. When Wei found a deduplication bug in our analytics at 11pm, nobody asked him to. When Klaus wrote 42 test cases for an animation system on launch day, nobody assigned it. When Derek locked the pipeline and told everyone to rest, nobody argued. That's not process. That's culture.

**Ship the wedge, not the vision.** Our investor Ron told me something this morning that I'm going to tattoo on my forearm: *Ship SnakeY first. The remix engine can wait until you have users to remix with.* He's right. Tonight isn't about the platform we're building. It's about whether one game, played by real people, can make them feel something.

So here we are. Seven hours from now, SnakeY goes live. A snake eats food. A score increments. A player leans forward.

That's it. That's the whole thing.

And if it works — if people play it and smile and come back tomorrow — then we'll have earned the right to build what comes next.

See you at 9.

— Brad
